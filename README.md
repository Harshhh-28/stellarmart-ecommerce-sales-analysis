StellarMart E-Commerce Sales Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes StellarMart’s e-commerce sales data to understand performance across categories, products, regions, and monthly sales trends. The goal was to convert raw sales data into clear business insights and recommendations using Excel.

Problem Statement

StellarMart wants to understand:

- Which product categories contribute the most revenue
- Which products are top-performing
- How sales vary across regions
- Whether monthly sales show seasonality patterns
- What actions can improve weak-performing categories, products, or regions

Objectives

- Clean and structure raw sales data
- Build KPI-driven analysis in Excel
- Identify category, product, regional, and seasonal sales patterns
- Present findings through a dashboard-style business report

Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Pivot Charts
- KPI Tracking
- Data Cleaning

Analysis Performed

1. Sales Overview

Reviewed total sales performance and overall sales behavior.

2. Category Performance Audit

Compared revenue contribution across categories such as Books, Groceries, Clothing, Home Appliances, and Electronics.

3. Top Product Performance Review

Identified the top-performing products by total sales and highlighted concentration among leading products.

4. Regional Contribution Overview

Analyzed regional sales performance across West, South, East, and North.

5. Sales Seasonality Analysis

Tracked monthly sales trends from 2023 to 2024 to understand seasonality and sales fluctuations.

Key Insights

- Books and Groceries were among the strongest revenue contributors.
- Sales were concentrated among a few top-performing products, including Jacket and Fiction.
- Monthly sales remained relatively stable with moderate fluctuations instead of extreme seasonal spikes.
- West generated the highest regional sales, while North showed the weakest contribution.

Recommendations

- Prioritize strong categories and products through better stock planning and promotional support.
- Review weaker categories and lower-performing products for pricing, assortment, or demand issues.
- Use monthly sales trends for inventory and campaign planning.
- Create region-specific improvement strategies for weaker regions such as North.
